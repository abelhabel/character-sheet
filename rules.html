Rules

Introduction

Fangalia is a tactical turn based game where two teams start on either side of a battle field and fight against each other until only one team is left.

A team is a composition of different monsters, which you pick before a battle starts. Each monster has stats and a set of abilities. These stats and abilities together make each monster unique. It is, then, a matter of picking monsters so that they together as a team enables a game tactic that suits you and that you think will bring victory to you.

Battle is done through four separate actions: moving, defending, waiting and/or using abilities. These actions are all actions that can be done in a monster's turn and are not exclusive to each other. It is possible, for example, to first move then use an ability.

These are only some high level concepts about the game and its rules but not all game concepts and terms have been introduced here, so please continue reading to learn more about specific concepts

Basics

Turn

During combat, each monster in either team gets to act when it is their turn to act. When a monster gets to act is dependent on the monster's Initiative stat. The higher a monster's Initiative the earlier it gets to act. More on that in the Initiative section.

When all monsters have used their turn a new Round starts.

Round

A round consists of many turns. Specifically, at the start of each round each monster gets a number of turns in that round based on their Inititive stat. It is possible for one monster to get more than one turn in a round.

A round is a measure of how long a battle has been going on but has not other significant impacts on the game.

Moving

On a monster's turn if it has a Movement stat above zero, the monster can move. The monster can move a number of tiles equal to its Movement stat. A move cannot be diagonal, meaning that if you want to move to an adjacent diagonal tile it will cost two movement.

On any move you are using the monster's movement as a resource, so moving one tile has used one movement that turn. This resource is replenished to full at the start of a monster's turn.

Moving does not end your turn. You can also make many small movements just as you can make one long movement in a turn, as long as you have not used up all your movement in that turn.

Use Ability

A monster can use as many abilities as their APT (Actions Per Turn) stat allows. Meaning that if a monster has an APT stat of 2, it can use two abilities (or one ability twice) in one turn.

Once a monster has used abilities equal to its APT stat, the monster's turn end.

Defend

At any point during a monster's turn, it can defend. Defending immediately ends the turn and adds a circumstantial bonus to the monster's Defense stat. If the monster has moved that turn, the bonus will be less. Defending always gives a Defense bonus of at least 2. This bonus stacks with other effects.

Wait

Waiting delays a monster's turn to later in the same round. This can be tactically useful if a monster has no targets within range, allowing for enemies to move within range or allies to make the monster's turn more favorable when acting later. A monster can only wait once per round. If a monster has performed any other action in that turn it cannot wait.

Terminology

Full Health

A monster is considered to be on full health when it has not taken any damage. A monster that first takes damage, but is then healed to full life, is considered to be on full health.

Wounded

A monster is considered to be wounded when it has less than 50% of max health.

Near Death

A monster is considered to be near death when it has less than 90% of max health.

Adjacent

Adjacent is a distance of 1. Diagonal tiles counts as 1. An enemy/ally is considered adjacent if it is at most 1 tile away.

Near By

Near by is a distance of 3. Diagonal tiles counts as 1. An enemy/ally is considered near by if it is at most 3 tiles away.

Far Away

Far away is a distance of 6 or more. Diagonal tiles counts as 1. An enemy/ally is considered far away if it is at least 6 tile away.

Battle

Flanking

A monster is considered flanked when two or more enemies are adjacent to it. For each adjacent enemy above one increases the damage taken by 20% when hit by attacks. It does not provide a damage bonus for spells.

Example 1:
A monster that is hit by an attack while 2 enemies are adjacent takes: (2 - 1) * 20% = 20%, extra damage.

Example 2:
A monster that is hit by an attack while 5 enemies are adjacent takes: (5 - 1) * 20% = 80%, extra damage.

Attack Roll

          
            stacks = number of stacks of the attacker
            attack = attackers Attack stat
            defense = defenders Defense stat

            if(attack > defense)
              multiplier = 1 + (attack - defense)/10;
            else
              multiplier = max(0.1, 1 + (attack - defense)/20);

            flanking multiplier = number of adjacent enemies - 1
            bonus damage = attackers Damage stat
            ailment multiplier = ailments on defender and attacker added together
            vigor multiplier = vigors on defender and attacker added together
            ability minimum damage = ability minimum power + bonus damage
            ability maximum damage = ability maximum power + bonus damage
            ability multiplier = ability power multiplier
            ability damage = random(ability minimum damage, ability maximum damage) * ability multiplier
            ------------------------------------
            total damage = ability damage * stacks * multiplier * flanking multiplier * vigor multiplier * ailment multiplier

Spell Roll

          
            spell resistance = defenders Spell Resistance stat
            resist roll = random(1, 100)
            if(resist roll < spell resistance)
              spell is resisted
            else

            stacks = number of stacks of the attacker
            spell power multipler = 1 + (attackers Spell Power stat / 10)
            bonus damage = attackers Damage stat
            ailment multiplier = ailments on defender and attacker added together
            vigor multiplier = vigors on defender and attacker added together
            ability minimum damage = ability minimum power + bonus damage
            ability maximum damage = ability maximum power + bonus damage
            ability multiplier = ability power multiplier
            ability damage = random(ability minimum damage, ability maximum damage) * ability multiplier
            ------------------------------------
            total damage = ability damage * stacks * spell power multipler * vigor multiplier * ailment multiplier

Abilities

Monsters use abilities in order to deal damage or provide bonuses and penalties to a monster's stats.

Type

There are three types of abilities: Active, Passive, and Trigger.

Active

An active ability requires you to first select the ability and then activate the ability on the battle field. Activating an active ability is different depending on the ability's Target stat.

Passive

A passive ability is always in effect (unless it's condition is not met). A passive ability can either effect yourself or other monsters around the monster that has the passive ability. This is controlled by an ability's Shape, Radius, and Target stats.

A passive ability that an area of effect is called an Aura.

Trigger

A trigger ability cannot be selected to be used and is not active all the time. Instead a trigger activates when a certain event happens. The type of event is decided by the ability's Activation stat.

These are the types of events that can trigger a trigger ability:
when attack hits, when self is hit, when adjacent ally is hit, when nearby ally is hit, when ally is hit, when adjacent enemy is hit, when nearyby enemy is hit, when enemy is hit, when adjacent enemy is slain, when enemy is slain by self

Condition

Some abilities require a condition to be met for the ability to be in effect.

These conditions are: flanked, flanking, wounded, near death, full health

Shape

The shape of an ability determines how it distributes over many tiles on the battle field.

These are the shapes an ability can have: point, line, cone, circle, square.

Radius

The radius of an ability determines how far it's shape reaches. In the case when an ability's shape is set to Line the radius determines the length of the line.

Target

The target consists of two pieces of information. The first, determines if you need to target a monster or if you need to target a tile, when you activate the ability on the battle field. The second piece of information determines what targets that will be hit.

Targets that can be selected are one of these: actor, ground, or self.

Targets that can be hit by the ability are one of these: allies, enemies, self, or all.

The formatting of the target stat will look like this ground/enemies, or actor/allies. Or more generally put: [what to select]/[who the ability will hit]

Range

The range of an ability decides how many tiles away from the monster that is using the ability you can target. A range of 5 will let you target 5 tiles away in a square fashion, ie tiles diagonal to the monster counts as one tile away.

An ability's shape has its origin where you target on the battle field. This means that if an ability has a range of 5, a shape of circle, and radius 3, the ability's area of effect will originate up to 5 tiles away in a circle with radius 3.

Duration

When an ability has a duration longer than 1, it will create en Effect. The Effect will then last for Turns equal to the duration. If the ability deals damage (Spell or Attack), then the damage will also be dealt at all subsequent rounds. If the ability affects a monster's stat, the effect will remain until the duration ends, ie the effect will not be reapplied on subsequent turns as is the case when the ability deals damage.

Resource Cost

Some abilities has a resource cost. It is either Mana or Health. If an ability cost 4 Mana but the monster only has 3 Mana available, then the ability cannot be used. The same logic applies if the resource is health.

Element

There are seven types of elements: force, fire, water, air, earth, vitality, rot

Force

The force element represent slashing, bludgening, will. The associated monster stats are: Attack, Damage

Fire

The fire element represent heat and burning damage, fury, anger, toughness. The associated monster stats are: Health, APT (Actions Per Turn)

Water

The water element represent water, ice, rain, tranquility, slowness. The associated monster stats are: Mana, Spell Power

Air

The air element represent wind, electricity, reflexes, quickness. The associated monster stats are: Movement, Initiative, TPT (Triggers Per Turn)

Earth

The earth element represent acid, poison, corrotion, roughness. The associated monster stats are: Defense, Spell Resistance,

Vitality

The vitality element represent life, divinity, hope, rejuvenation.

Vitality damage always heals monsters rather than damaging them, except for the Undead.

Rot

The rot element represent decay, death, revulsion.

Rot heals the Undead but deals damage as the other elements to all other monster families.

Power

The power of an ability is what determines how much damage it does in the case of Attacks and Spells, or how much the bonus is in case of Blessings, or how much the penalty is in the case of Curses.

The power consists of a minimum and maximum and a value between these two is then randomly chosen when the ability is used. This value is then also multiplied by the abilities multiplier. All these values are listed on all abilities.

The monster's Damage stat is always added to the minimum and maximum power of an ability when the ability is an Attack or a Spell.

Stacks

Stacking occurs when more than one of the same effect is applied at one time. However, there are specific rules for when effects stack and when they don't.

Effects stack during the following conditions:

  • If the effect is a Spell or an Attack AND they have different sources. Example: Fire Ball and Dragon Fire, Flaming Arrow and Blizzard
  • If the effects are from the same source AND the source is able to stack with itself. Example: Weakening Bite, Decaying Shot

Effects do not stack during the following conditions:

  • If the effect is a Blessing or a Curse AND they are from different sources. Example: Aura of Corrotion and Corrode, Shield Wall and Armoured

Source

There are four different sources an ability can have: Attack, Spell, Blessing, Curse

Attacks usually deal damage which is determined by a Damage Roll.

Spells usually deal damage but can also have some special effect, such as teleport. Spell damage is determined by a Spell Roll.

Blessings always gives a bonus to a monster's stat.

Curses always gives a penalty to a monster's stat.

Ailment

An ailment is a special condition that applies to a monster and they are always negative. These are the ailments: stunned, held, overwhelmed, meekend, exposed, rushed.

Some ailments are only applied when hit by an element. These are the ailments for the elements:
Force: bruised, bledding
Fire: singed, scorched
Water: wet, soaked
Air: winded, shocked
Earth: contagious, brittle
Vitality: dazzled, blinded
Rot: wilted, blighted

Stunned

A monster that is stunned is unable to take any action, making it automatically lose its turn.

Held

A monster that is held is unable to move, regardless of any bonuses to Movement stat.

Overwhelmed

A monster that is overwhelmed takes 25% increased damage from monsters that are adjacent.

Meekend

A monster that is meekend deals 25% reduced damage to targets that are adjacent.

Exposed

A monster that is exposed takes 25% increased damage from monsters that are far away.

Rushed

A monster that is rushed deals 25% reduced damage to targets that are far away.

Bruised

A monster that is bruised takes 25% increased force damage.

Bleeding

A monster that is bleeding takes 2% of current life for each tile moved. If 2% is less than 1, the monster still takes 1 damage.

Singed

A monster that is singed takes 25% increased fire damage.

Scorched

A monster that is scorched has all mana costs for abilites increased by 1.

Wet

A monster that is wet has all movement penalties from curses increased by 1 and all movement bonuses from blessings decreased by 1.

Soaked

A monster that is soaked deals 25% reduced spell damage.

Winded

A monster that is winded cannot trigger abilities.

Shocked

A monster that is shocked cannot use more than one ability per turn, effectivly setting the monster's APT to 1.

Contagious

A monster that is contagious spreads curses and earth damage over time to its adjacent allies at the start of its turn.

Brittle

A monster that is brittle has a 50% reduced base defense stat.

Dazzled

A monster that is dazzled is only healed for 50%, when healing is received.

Blinded

A monster that is blinded cannot receive new blessings.

Wilted

A monster that is wilted takes damage whenevever it uses mana. The damage taken is equal to 5 times the mana used.

Blighted

A monster that is blighted instead takes damage from vitality damage, rather than being healed. Blighted has no effect on the undead.

Vigor

A vigor is a special condition that applies to a monster and they are always positive. These are the vigors: intimidating, prepared, precise, hidden

Intimidating

A monster that is intimidating deals 25% increased damage to monsters that are adjacent.

Prepared

A monster that is prepared takes 25% reduced damage from targets that are adjacent.

Precise

A monster that is precise deals 25% increased damage to monsters that are far away.

Hidden

A monster that is hidden takes 25% reduced damage from targets that are far away.

Illuminated

A monster that is illuminated has bonus damage equal to half the number of blessings present on it, rounded down. This bonus is in addition to any other bonuses to damage. Blessings from passive abilities and auras do not count towards the bonus.

Engorged

A monster that is engorged deals 50% increased spell damage.

Charged

A monster that is charged has a 30% additional chance of inflicting elemental ailments when dealing damage.

Monsters

A monster is the central game mechanic, much like a chess piece is central to chess. Likewise, just as there are different chess pieces there are different monsters.

Each monster is unique in the sense that the combination of its stats and its abilities make them unique. Some monsters can stack.

Family

A monster belongs to a family. You can think of this as a faction, group etc. A family of monsters represents a theme of both flavor and tactics. Outlaws focus on attacks but Demons focus more on spells, for example.

Cost

When selecting a team to do battle with, you have a maximum amount of cash to spend. Each monster has a cost which is then subtracted from your available cash if you add it to your team. The cost represent one unit of that monster.

Max Stacks

Some monsters can stack together to form one battle unit made of multiple of the same monster. The max stacks says what the maximum any given monster can stack up to.

The Bronze Dragon has a max stack of 1, so you can never have more than one Bronze Dragon in a battle unit. The Imp, can however stack up to 20 times, meaning that one battle unit can consist of up to 20 Imps.

Health

The health of a monster determines how much damage can receive before it dies. A monster is dead if it has zero or lower health.

Mana

Mana is the main resource a monster use for using abilities. If a monster does not have any mana it cannot use abilities that have a mana cost of 1 or more.

Attack

When a monster uses an ability with source: attack, the monster uses its attack stat to determine how much damage is done to the target.

An attacker's attack stat is always relative to a defenders defense stat when determining how much damage is inflicted on the defender. The higher the attack stat the higher the damage. The damage is calculated by the attack roll.

Defense

When a monster is being attacked by an ability with source: attack, the monster uses its defense stat to determine how much damage is done to it.

A defenders defense stat is always relative to an attackers attack stat when determining how much damage is inflicted on the defender. The higher the attack stat the higher the damage. The damage is calculated by the attack roll.

Spell Power

When a monster uses an ability with source: spell, the monster uses its spell power stat to determine how much damage is done to the target. The damage is calculated by the spell roll.

Spell Resistance

When a monster is hit by an ability with source: spell, the monster uses its spell resistance stat to determine if it resists the spell it will resist the spell completely, meaning that the ability will have zero effect.

A spell resistance stat of 100 means that the monster is immune to all spells, even beneficial spells.

Damage

The damage stat gives bonus to the minimum and maximum damage of attack and spell abilities.

APT (Actions Per Turn)

The APT says how many abilities that a monster can use per turn.

Even if a monster has more than one turn in a round, it still gets to use an amount of abilities on that turn equal to its APT stat.

TPT (Triggers Per Turn)

The APT says how many abilities that can be triggered per turn.

Even if a monster has more than one turn in a round, it still gets to use an amount of triggered abilities on that turn equal to its TPT stat.

A triggered ability is counted per target hit, ie if a monster casts a Fire Ball that hits 4 targets but the monster only has a TPT stat of 3, it can only trigger 3 triggered abilities. The abilities themselves, however, can hit as many targets as the ability allows.

Movement

The movement stat determines how many tiles a monster can move in a turn.

Initiative

A monster's initiative determines how early a monster gets its turn in a round. When a monster's initiative is greater than 20, the monster will get more than one turn per round in certain rounds.

Initiative also determines how manu turns a monster gets over 20 rounds. If a monster has an initiaitve of 31 it will get 31 turns over 20 rounds, or initiative of 24 gives 24 turns over 20 rounds. However, a monster always gets at least 1 turn per rounds, so a monster that has an initiative of 4 still gets 1 turn per round, or 20 turns over 20 rounds.

It is also possible to calculate when the monster will get those extra turns if the initiative is greater than 20, keeping track of a turn counter with these rules :
1. For turn, add (initiative - 20) to the turn counter.
2. When the turn counter is greater than 10, gain an extra turn.
3. When an extra turn is gained, subtract 20 from the turn counter.

Example:
Monster initiative is 25.
Round 1, Turn 1: turn counter = 0.
- Add (25 - 20) to turn counter, turn counter = 0 + 5 = 5.
turn counter is less than 10. No extra turn this round.

Round 2, Turn 1: turn counter = 5.
- Add (25 - 20) to turn counter, turn counter = 5 + 5 = 10.
turn counter is less than 10. No extra turn this round.

Round 3, Turn 1: turn counter = 10.
- Add (25 - 20) to turn counter, turn counter = 10 + 5 = 15.
turn counter is more than 10. Gain extra turn!
- Subtract 20 from the turn counter, turn counter = 15 - 20 = -5.

As can be seen, the monster will gain an extra turn on round 3. If you continue the same logic into future rounds, you can see that the monster will get extra turns on rounds 7, 11, 15, 19 etc.
